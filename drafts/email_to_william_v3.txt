Most cold-email tools optimize for volume — send more, hope harder. The real problem isn't writing the first email; it's the inbox AFTER. You spend 30 minutes a day on thoughtful first-touch and three hours composing replies that say "yes, here's the pricing" or "good question, the answer is X."

Salesman flips that. The reply-handling does itself.

When a prospect responds, the system reads the whole thread, figures out what they actually want (pricing, a meeting, an objection answered), and drafts the response — not just classifies it. You click approve. Same gates as the cold side: a detector ensemble that catches generic copy, a human-approval requirement, a signed receipt. You get the inbox down to five minutes a day, and every reply gets answered fast and answered well. That's where deals close.
